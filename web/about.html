Why I Built ORQL

ORQL was born from a deep dissatisfaction with the gap between quantum formalism and lived experience.

I wanted a computable, symbolic system that reflects how observers actually experience measurement ‚Äî not just as abstract math, but as a process shaped by memory, entanglement, and informational context.

I built ORQL because:

  • I reject the idea that there's one objective, global reality
  • Physics needs to account for the observer, not just the system
  • Existing interpretations were either incomplete or failed to match lived experience
  • We need a bridge between mathematical structure and subjective perception
ORQL exists to reframe quantum mechanics around observers, not abstractions ‚Äî to formalize subjective collapse, divergence, and meaning without sacrificing logic. It's a new language for a reality that's always been relational.
